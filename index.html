Project Pitch

JamBuddy

your real-time musical co-pilot

Problem

You can practice. But are you improving?

Tuners tell you pitch.

Metronomes tell you timing.

But nothing listens to what you're actually playing and responds in real time.

What If..

your phone could hear music the way a musician does?

Musicians don't have magical ears, but they have practice. They don't just hear sound — they hear a G minor chord resolving to a C major, a melody sitting in the Dorian mode, a rhythm pushing slightly ahead of the beat. That instant, intuitive analysis is what separates a trained ear.

JamBuddy brings the musician's ear to your pocket. Using real-time audio analysis, it listens the way a musician listens, and understands harmony and context as it happens.

JamBuddy — Visualise and understand the sounds from instruments.

JamBuddy instantly tells you what you're playing — the chord, the key, the scale, the progression building under your fingers.

No setup, no music theory knowledge required. You pick up your guitar, you play, and JamBuddy keeps up. It's not a tuner, it's not a lesson — it's a practice companion that understands your music in real time, so you can focus on playing instead of analyzing.

For Who?

We live in the age of information. Every chord, every scale, every technique has a tutorial. Learning to play has never been more accessible — and yet, millions of musicians hit a wall.

They can hold the guitar. They can strum the chords. They've memorized a few songs. But they can't move forward.

JamBuddy is built for them — because for the first time, there's something on the other end that actually listens.

Intermediates are the biggest group, while being the most overlooked.

Ok, it listens,

Then what?

Listening is just the beginning. JamBuddy takes what it hears and makes the invisible visible — the patterns, the harmonic connections, the relationships between notes that a trained musician feels intuitively but a learner can't yet see.

* focus ring Displays coherent chords together
* musical mode Hints the mood / emotion
* chord numerals Helps with progression creation
